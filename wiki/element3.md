# Reputation, Badges & Moderation

Every community needs a way to recognize its best contributors and keep things running smoothly. Curio's reputation system does both — rewarding helpful members with visible status, while giving moderators the tools they need to maintain a healthy, safe environment.

What makes Curio's system unique is that **reputation only ever goes up**. No downvotes, no point deductions, no griefing. The entire system is built to encourage positive participation without punishing mistakes.

Here is a simple, feature-first guide to how reputation, badges, and moderation work in Curio.

---

## What Makes Curio Unique?

1. **Upward-Only Reputation**: Points are earned through contributions and answers. Downvotes never deduct points, so users never fear participating. The floor is always zero.
2. **Automatic Badge Progression**: Badges unlock automatically as you earn points — no applications, no nominations, no bureaucracy.
3. **Expert-to-Moderator Pipeline**: Reach the Expert tier and Curio automatically invites you to apply for moderator privileges. The best contributors naturally become the next stewards.
4. **Layered Safety System**: From automated spam escalation to admin-verified answers, Curio protects quality at every level without relying on a single point of control.

---

## The Guided Tour of Reputation Features

Here is how earning, recognition, and moderation work in Curio, explained in simple terms:

### 1. Earning Reputation Points

Curio rewards users for actions that make the community better. You earn points in three main ways:

- **Answer Accepted**: When someone marks your answer as helpful, you earn the biggest reputation bonus. This is the highest form of recognition in Curio.
- **Answer Liked**: The question author can upvote answers. Each upvote gives you a smaller but meaningful boost.
- **Query Resolved**: Even asking questions has value. When your question is resolved and closed, you earn points for contributing to the knowledge base.

Points are cumulative and never decrease — there are no penalties or deductions.

### 2. Badge Tiers & Recognition

Curio automatically awards badges as you accumulate points. Your badge appears next to your name throughout the platform, signaling your experience level to the community.

| Tier | What It Means |
| --- | --- |
| **Newcomer** | Everyone starts here. You're exploring the platform and learning how things work. |
| **Helper** | You've helped solve a few questions. The community recognizes you as someone who contributes. |
| **Contributor** | You're a regular, reliable source of answers. Other users benefit from your knowledge regularly. |
| **Expert** | You're one of the top knowledge holders. At this tier, Curio invites you to apply for moderator status. |
| **Legend** | The highest honor. You have made an exceptional impact on the community's knowledge base. |

The top badge you've earned is displayed prominently under your name everywhere in Curio — on questions, answers, and in the user directory.

### 3. The Expert-to-Moderator Path

Reaching the Expert tier unlocks more than just a badge. Curio automatically sends you a system notification inviting you to apply for moderator access. You can accept the invitation from your Settings page, which flags your profile for admin review. Once approved, you gain all the powers needed to help maintain the community.

### 4. Admin-Verified Answers

Sometimes an answer is not just helpful — it's authoritative. Admins can mark any answer as "verified," which gives it a special badge and bumps it to the top of the answer list. Unlike other badges, the Verified badge is permanent — it stays on your profile even if the answer is later changed, ensuring your verified contributions are always recognized.

### 5. The Spam Defense System

Curio doesn't leave safety to chance. A built-in spam escalation ladder automatically monitors for problematic behavior:

- **First strike**: A warning is logged. No badge, just a notice.
- **Second strike**: A Warning badge is issued, and the user is temporarily banned for 24 hours.
- **Fifth strike**: The account is Restricted — all future posts require admin approval before going live.
- **Tenth strike**: The account is permanently Suspended.

The system applies the most severe penalty your behavior has reached — so there's no way to "reset" the ladder.

### 6. Negative Badges (Admin-Issued)

Admins can also manually issue negative badges to users who violate community standards:

- **Warning**: Informational only — a public notice that behavior needs to change.
- **Restricted**: All posts require admin clearance before they appear. The user can still participate, but everything is filtered.
- **Suspended**: Permanent removal from the platform. The account is locked and cannot be recovered.

Every badge issuance is automatically logged with full details — who issued it, when, and why — and the affected user receives a notification.

### 7. The Moderation Queue (Flag Review)

When content is flagged — whether by users or by automated detection — it lands in the moderation queue. Flags are organized by type:

- **Duplicate**: A question was posted that matches an existing thread.
- **Report**: A user manually reported content.
- **Spam**: Auto-detected as promotional or abusive.
- **Outdated**: Content that no longer reflects current information.
- **Gibberish**: Auto-detected as nonsensical or low-quality.

Admins can **Resolve** a flag (confirm it and close the issue), **Dismiss** it (clear it without action), or **Merge** duplicates — which automatically transfers all answers to the canonical thread, archives the duplicate, and notifies the author.

### 8. The Attention Escalation Queue

Sometimes a question needs an admin's eyes even though nothing is "wrong" with it. Expert-tier users and moderators can flag questions that need admin attention. These appear in a special queue grouped by category and sorted by urgency, showing the asker's details so admins can quickly assess and resolve the situation. Once handled, the flag is cleared with a single click.

### 9. Moderator Powers

Moderators are trusted community members with elevated privileges. Whether they earned the role through the Expert pipeline or were directly appointed by an admin, moderators can:

- Delete any question or answer (with a 15-minute undo window)
- Restore accidentally deleted content
- Edit a question's category and tags to keep things organized
- Rate answers on any question
- Clear comments on any thread
- Flag questions for admin attention

A dedicated moderator roster shows all active moderators and their reputation points, so the community always knows who helps keep things running.

### 10. The Audit Trail

Trust requires transparency. Every moderation action — every badge issued, every flag resolved, every role change — is recorded in an immutable, timestamped audit log. The log shows exactly who did what and when, creating a complete history that keeps the entire moderation team accountable.

---

## Keeping the Community Healthy

Curio's reputation and moderation system is designed to be self-reinforcing. Good contributors rise naturally through the ranks. Problematic behavior is caught early and escalated proportionally. Moderators are drawn from the most experienced members, not appointed from outside. And every action is logged for full transparency.

The result is a community that polices itself with warmth, not fear — because reputation only goes up, and everyone has a path to becoming a trusted steward.
